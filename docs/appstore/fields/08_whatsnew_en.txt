This update brings full iPad support and more accurate gesture typing.

[New]
- Full iPad support: an iPad-optimized layout with a split keyboard in both landscape and portrait. In landscape, a number pad sits on one side and the Korean keyboard on the other, so you can type numbers without switching layers; place the number pad on the left or right. Keyboard height adapts to your screen size.
- Vowels straight from consonants: swipe a consonant key diagonally (vertical or horizontal lead) and keep going to complete the vowel. For example, swiping up-right then right makes a vowel like the one in 'ga', and down-left then up makes the one in 'go'.

[Accuracy improvements]
- Fine-tune the 8-direction recognition angle for each direction, left and right independently.
- New 4-way-only mode that disables diagonals.
- Adjustable sensitivity for back-and-forth vowels.
- A live gesture tester shows how your swipes are recognized so you can calibrate.
- Follow-up strokes are recognized more accurately, even after small jitters or long entry motions.

Core typing works without Full Access. Haptic feedback requires iOS Full Access (optional); your input is never transmitted off your device even when it is enabled.
